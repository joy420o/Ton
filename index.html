HISTORY

In 1861, Michael Thonet was the best tree for making bentwood
furniture, a tree with many jade trees

Thanks to the environment surrounded by jade trees,
The supply and demand of materials were easy, and the geogra
phical location also reduced labor and transportation costs

Since 1912, it has sold more than 445,000 products each year,
with 2,000 employees

HOW WE MAKE IT

1 Wood drying and cutting operations

The warehouse holds more than 300 different types of wood cut for different purposes.
After grading based on the color of the wood, it is managed for about 5 months to maintain
the moisture content of the wood at a certain level.
In summer, the wood is wetted occasionally and in winter, the wood is dried in a kiln.
It is a necessary process for the durability and high quality of wood.
